# Music Distribution Guide

> How to get your music on every streaming platform - options, costs, and recommendations

## Overview

Music distribution is how your songs get from your computer to Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon, and 150+ other platforms. This guide covers your options and how HLPFL can help.

---

## Distribution Options

### 1. DIY Distributors (Self-Service)

These are platforms where you upload your music yourself and they distribute to streaming services.

#### **DistroKid** - Recommended for Most Artists
- **Cost**: $22.99/year (unlimited uploads)
- **Pros**:
  - Unlimited releases for one annual fee
  - Fast delivery (1-2 days to most platforms)
  - Keep 100% of royalties
  - Spotify for Artists access
  - Lyrics distribution
  - YouTube Content ID
- **Cons**:
  - Music removed if you stop paying
  - Limited customer support
  - No advances or marketing support
- **Best For**: Active artists releasing frequently
- **Website**: [distrokid.com](https://distrokid.com)

#### **TuneCore**
- **Cost**: $9.99/single, $29.99/album per year
- **Pros**:
  - Industry reputation
  - Publishing administration available
  - YouTube monetization
  - Detailed analytics
- **Cons**:
  - Per-release pricing gets expensive
  - Annual renewal fees for each release
- **Best For**: Artists with fewer releases
- **Website**: [tunecore.com](https://tunecore.com)

#### **CD Baby**
- **Cost**: $9.95/single (one-time), $29/album (one-time)
- **Pros**:
  - One-time fee (music stays up forever)
  - Physical distribution options
  - Sync licensing program
  - Publishing administration
- **Cons**:
  - Takes 9% of streaming royalties
  - Slower delivery times
- **Best For**: Artists who want "set and forget"
- **Website**: [cdbaby.com](https://cdbaby.com)

#### **Amuse**
- **Cost**: Free tier available, Pro at $5/month
- **Pros**:
  - Free distribution option
  - A&R discovery program
  - Fast payouts
- **Cons**:
  - Free tier has limitations
  - Smaller platform reach on free tier
- **Best For**: New artists testing the waters
- **Website**: [amuse.io](https://amuse.io)

#### **LANDR**
- **Cost**: $12-36/year depending on plan
- **Pros**:
  - Includes mastering credits
  - Cover art creation tools
  - Collaboration features
- **Cons**:
  - Mastering quality varies
  - Can get pricey for features
- **Best For**: Artists who need mastering + distribution
- **Website**: [landr.com](https://landr.com)

### 2. Label Services / White-Glove Distribution

These distributors offer more services but take a percentage of royalties.

#### **AWAL** (Artist Without A Label)
- **Cost**: Apply only (selective)
- **Revenue Split**: 80% to artist
- **Pros**:
  - Marketing and playlist pitching support
  - Advances for qualifying artists
  - Major label connections
  - Premium analytics
- **Cons**:
  - Selective (need existing fanbase)
  - Takes 20% of royalties
- **Best For**: Established independent artists
- **Website**: [awal.com](https://awal.com)

#### **The Orchard** (Sony)
- **Cost**: Apply only
- **Revenue Split**: Varies (negotiated)
- **Pros**:
  - Major label backing
  - Global reach
  - Significant marketing resources
- **Cons**:
  - Very selective
  - May require multi-year deals
- **Best For**: Artists ready for major label attention
- **Website**: [theorchard.com](https://theorchard.com)

#### **Believe/TuneCore Pro**
- **Cost**: Apply only
- **Revenue Split**: Negotiated
- **Pros**:
  - Full-service label support
  - Advances available
  - Strong international presence
- **Cons**:
  - Selective
  - Complex contracts
- **Best For**: International artists
- **Website**: [believemusic.com](https://believemusic.com)

### 3. Aggregators for Labels

If you're building a label or managing multiple artists:

#### **Symphonic Distribution**
- **Cost**: Plans from $19.99/year
- **Pros**:
  - Label services
  - Publishing administration
  - Sync licensing
- **Best For**: Small labels

#### **ONErpm**
- **Cost**: Revenue share model
- **Pros**:
  - Artist development programs
  - Marketing services
  - YouTube network
- **Best For**: Growing labels

## What You Need Before Distributing

### Required:
- [ ] High-quality audio files (WAV, 16-bit, 44.1kHz minimum)
- [ ] Album artwork (3000x3000 pixels, JPG or PNG)
- [ ] ISRC codes (most distributors provide free)
- [ ] UPC/EAN code (most distributors provide free)
- [ ] Metadata (song titles, artist name, genre, release date)

### Recommended:
- [ ] Spotify for Artists claim ready
- [ ] Apple Music for Artists claim ready
- [ ] Pre-save campaign planned
- [ ] Social media announcement scheduled
- [ ] Press/blog outreach prepared

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Spotify
- Submit to playlists 4+ weeks before release via Spotify for Artists
- Use canvas videos for engagement
- Claim your profile immediately after first release

### Apple Music
- Takes longest to review (plan 2 weeks)
- Spatial Audio available for qualifying tracks
- Lyrics sync available

### YouTube Music
- Content ID recommended for monetization
- Official Artist Channel requires verification
- Art tracks generated automatically

### TikTok/Instagram
- Most distributors include free
- 60-second clips distributed
- Can drive massive streaming

## Revenue Timeline

Typical payment schedules from streaming:
- **Spotify**: Pays distributors monthly, 2-3 month delay
- **Apple Music**: Pays monthly, 2 month delay
- **Amazon**: Pays monthly, 2-3 month delay
- **Distributors to You**: Monthly or quarterly

**Expect 3-4 months from stream to payment.**
